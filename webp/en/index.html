What is a WebP image?

The WebP file format by Google

WebP is a modern image format developed by Google in 2010. It offers both lossy and lossless compression, more efficient than JPG for photos and more efficient than PNG for graphics with transparency.

However, many editing programs, e-commerce platforms and social networks still do not accept WebP files natively, making conversion to JPG or PNG necessary.

Convert from WebP, when do I need it?

  • When you download images from Google and they appear in WebP format
  • When you need to send the image to someone using older software
  • When a platform does not accept WebP for profile pictures or product images
  • When you need to edit the image in Photoshop or similar programs

Convert to WebP, when do I need it?

  • To improve your website loading speed (Core Web Vitals)
  • To reduce bandwidth consumption
  • To meet Google PageSpeed Insights recommendations

What is the difference between WebP, JPG and PNG?

JPG is ideal for photos: lossy compression, small file, widely compatible. PNG is ideal for graphics with transparency: lossless, larger file. WebP combines the best of both: superior compression and transparency support.
